Executive Summary

Over 90 days, the We Must Vote Record-Clearing Access Pilot reached 1,248 people in Harris County — residents facing barriers tied to old records in employment, housing, and civic participation. Of those who completed screening, 66% appeared likely eligible for some form of record relief.

The pilot demonstrated that a structured, technology-supported access workflow can move people from first awareness through filing readiness at a scale that ad hoc clinics and referral programs cannot match. We Must Vote now has a live infrastructure, a complete data record, and a documented case for expansion.

Lessons & Expansion
Section 3
What We Learned
3.1Operational Lessons
Targeted outreach significantly outperforms broad social posts
Email campaigns and staff referrals drove 60% of referrals and converted at nearly 3× the rate of general social media traffic. Future campaigns should invest heavily in direct and staff-mediated outreach.
Wilma reduces confusion and increases completion
User support themes showed early concerns about eligibility language and process expectations. Wilma's plain-English guidance reduced drop-off at the screening stage and kept intake completion high.
Case documentation is the biggest barrier after screening
191 likely eligible users did not start the expungement workflow. The primary reason was not confusion or disinterest — it was not having case numbers and charge information on hand. A "What You Need Before You Start" pre-screening page would have converted 30–40% of this group.
Fee barriers require a solution before scale
44 users with completed packets could not file due to filing fees. A fee waiver workflow or funder-backed filing fee fund should be part of the next-phase plan for Harris County.
The mid-funnel is the strongest part of the system
Once users entered the Expungement.ai workflow, 80% completed their packet. The technology works. The bottlenecks are at entry (documentation) and exit (filing fees).
3.2Recommendations for We Must Vote
1
Build a filing fee waiver workflow. Partner with Harris County legal aid or a funder to cover filing fees for income-eligible users. This alone could convert the 44 stuck users and prevent the same barrier in a renewal.
2
Add a case documentation pre-step to outreach. Before people click through to the intake, tell them what they need. A one-page "what to gather" guide distributed at community events could unlock 60+ additional completions per cycle.
3
Shift outreach investment from social to staff referrals and email. Staff referrals converted at 3× the rate of social posts. Training and incentivizing staff to refer is the highest-leverage outreach investment for the next cycle.
4
Re-engage the 191 likely eligible users who did not start. These users completed screening and were identified as likely eligible — they are warm. A targeted re-engagement campaign (email + SMS) could move a meaningful share of this group into the workflow immediately.
