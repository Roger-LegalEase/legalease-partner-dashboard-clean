# Mississippi RCAP Knowledge Pack

Phase 19 is Mississippi-only. The system does not enable a national document generator and does not add Georgia, Illinois, Maryland, Pennsylvania, DC, or any other state.

## Core Rules

- Mississippi does not have one single expungement pathway.
- Case outcome determines the pathway first.
- Non-conviction matters are treated differently from conviction matters.
- Misdemeanor conviction review is different from felony conviction review.
- Felony conviction review requires excluded-offense screening.
- For felony conviction petitions, the five-year clock may run from successful completion of all terms and conditions, not just the conviction date.
- Filing is generally in the court of origin.
- The predictable statutory filing fee is generally $150 per petition.
- Clerk copy, certification, and retrieval costs can vary.
- Court records and/or Mississippi DPS/CIC record requests may be needed.
- Mississippi does not provide one single statewide official form for this workflow; this system uses a draft pleading/template approach.
- Local practice can vary by court and clerk.
- County/court instructions support Circuit Court, County Court, Justice Court, Municipal Court, conviction/non-conviction differences, felony district attorney notice reminders, and local procedure verification.
- Automatic expungement proposals should not be treated as active unless current law is verified.

## Pathways

Pathway A: Dismissed / Non-Conviction

- Arrest then released and case dismissed
- Charges dropped or nolle prosequi
- No disposition
- Not guilty or acquittal
- Non-adjudication completed and dismissed
- Draft type: Petition for Dismissal and Expungement of Criminal Record

Pathway B: Misdemeanor Conviction

- First-offender misdemeanor conviction
- Non-traffic misdemeanor
- Sentence, fines, and costs completed
- Draft type: Petition for Expungement of Criminal Record

Pathway C: Felony Conviction

- One felony conviction
- All sentence terms completed
- Five-year waiting period may be relevant
- Excluded offense categories must be reviewed
- District attorney notice is required before hearing
- Draft type: Petition for Expungement of Criminal Record of Criminal Conviction

## Safety

All user-facing output must stay plain English and must avoid guarantees. Use language such as "may," "might," "possible pathway," "record review may help," and "this does not guarantee eligibility or outcomes."

The packet is a draft/preparation aid. It is not legal advice, not a final filing, and not a guarantee of court acceptance.
