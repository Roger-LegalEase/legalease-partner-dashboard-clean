# Illinois RCAP Knowledge Pack

Phase 19I is Illinois-only. It does not create a national document generator and does not add Georgia, Maryland, Pennsylvania, DC, or any other state.

## Core Rules

- Illinois is fundamentally expungement vs. sealing.
- Expungement is generally for non-convictions, supervision, qualified probation, reversed or vacated convictions, factual innocence, pardon with expungement authorization, and certain cannabis paths.
- Sealing is generally for eligible convictions that cannot be expunged.
- Sealing hides records from most public view but does not erase all government or law-enforcement access.
- Illinois uses mandatory statewide standardized forms accepted by Illinois circuit courts.
- Filing is generally in the county where the arrest or charge occurred.
- Cook County can have local district and filing-practice quirks.
- Agencies generally have a 60-day objection window.
- Agencies may have up to 60 days to process an order after entry.
- Fees vary by county; describe costs as a range, not a fixed quote.
- Fee waiver information should be surfaced for users who cannot afford filing costs.
- RAP sheet / ISP Access and Review is important before filing.
- Cannabis automatic expungement is special and should be verified before the user pays to petition.
- Clean Slate automatic sealing does not begin until 2029 and phases in through 2034.

## Pathways

Pathway A: Expungement - non-conviction.

Pathway B: Expungement - court supervision or qualified probation.

Pathway C: Sealing - eligible misdemeanor or felony conviction.

Pathway D: Excluded / needs review.

## Safety

All user-facing output must stay plain English and avoid guarantees. Use language such as "may," "might," "possible pathway," "record review may help," and "this does not guarantee eligibility or outcomes."

The packet is a draft/preparation aid. It is not legal advice, not a final filing, and not a guarantee of expungement, sealing, court acceptance, or any outcome.
