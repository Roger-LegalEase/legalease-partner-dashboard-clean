Statement of Points and Authorities in Support

I. Introduction. Movant seeks to seal the records of the above case under D.C. Code § 16-806, which authorizes the Court to seal records where the movant demonstrates, by a preponderance of the evidence, that sealing is in the interests of justice. Movant seeks to expunge the records of the above matter under D.C. Code § 16-803, which requires the Court to order expungement on grounds of actual innocence where the movant demonstrates, by a preponderance of the evidence, that the offense did not occur or was not committed by the movant.

II. Eligibility. The case reached a final disposition; the applicable § 16-806(a)(3) waiting period (measured from completion of sentence, which under § 16-801 does not require payment of monetary penalties) has elapsed or been waived; the offense is not in Master Grid Severity Group 1–3 and is not otherwise ineligible; and Movant has no open charges or disqualifying record. The case was terminated without a conviction, satisfying the threshold requirement of § 16-803(a)(1). There is no waiting period for actual-innocence expungement, so the motion is timely.

III. Argument.

IV. Conclusion. For the foregoing reasons, Movant respectfully requests that the Court grant the motion and enter the attached proposed Order.

How to file in DC (screen reference — not printed)

  1. Call (202) 727-4245 to request your arrest history report from the Metropolitan Police Department (MPD).
  2. Request your case disposition from DC Superior Court: email criminalcustomerservice@dcsc.gov or go to the Criminal Division clerk's office, 500 Indiana Ave NW, Room 4001.
  3. Prepare this motion package (motion, points & authorities, proposed order, certificate of service) with your arrest record and disposition attached.
  4. Email everything to the Seal Team at criminalmotionsealteam@dcsc.gov. They assign a case number and a judge. The process can take up to six months.
Free help: Legal Aid DC (202-628-1161) · Neighborhood Legal Services Program (202-832-6577) · Public Defender Service — ask for the Duty Day Attorney for Seal cases (202-824-2801) · Rising for Justice (202-638-4798) · Christian Legal Society (202-710-0592).
